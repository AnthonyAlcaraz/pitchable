Pitchable generates professional pitch decks from a brief, an uploaded document, or a topic. You configure the pitch lens -- audience (investors, customers, internal teams), goal (fundraising, sales, partnership), tone (formal, conversational, bold), and framework (Problem/Solution, MECE, Narrative arc) -- then the AI structures your content and renders it into slides using Marp CLI. Edit any slide via natural language chat: "Make slide 3 shorter." "Add a competitive landscape slide." Export to PPTX or PDF. Images generated via Nano Banana 2. Stack: NestJS + Prisma + PostgreSQL + BullMQ + Marp CLI + Claude AI. I built Pitchable because I spent 11 hours making a deck that got ignored in 4 minutes.

The core idea: you drop in a brief, paste a document, or type a topic. Pitchable extracts the key entities (market, problem, solution, traction) and generates a full slide deck in 2-15 minutes. Honest limitations: AI sometimes hallucinates market size numbers (always verify). Layouts are Markdown-based, not pixel-perfect. Generation takes 2-15 minutes depending on complexity.
